Inflation increased significantly in New York State, affecting residents' daily life

World inflation rate increased rapidly after Covid-19 pandemic. The constant price increase is caused by political instability in the world, the war in Ukraine and the global pandemic.

Prices in the New York City area edged up 0.2 percent for the second consecutive month in September. Rent prices are getting higher and people can't afford it, so they should move and search for a low quality apartment.

Over the year, the index for all items increased 4.9 percent. The October food index increased 0.6 percent, with prices for food at home and food away from home both increasing 0.6 percent. Four of the six grocery categories recorded increases.

Shelter prices, up 4.1 percent, included a 4.2 percent increase for owners’ equivalent rent and a 3.3 percent increase for residential rent. Medical care prices increased 7.9 percent. Prices for new and used motor vehicles were up 12.9 percent.
